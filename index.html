Singular Learning Theory

Watanabe's Singular Learning Theory is a mathematical theory of Bayesian statistical learning, general enough to apply to singular models such as neural networks. When studying a model's asymptotics, the learning coefficient (a.k.a. real log canonical threshold) takes the place of parameter counting, leading to useful information criteria such as the WAIC and WBIC.

A core object of the theory, and key to the interpretation of the learning coefficient in terms of volume scaling, is the state-density function. I wrote a note — The asymptotic expansion of the state-density function via the V-filtration — which derives its asymptotic expansion directly from the theory of D-modules.

PhD

University of Melbourne, 2022. Supervised by Daniel Murfet. Thesis

My thesis classified the finite-dimensional irreducible representations of the subregular finite W-algebras in simply-laced type, drawing on singularity theory, the representation theory of nilpotent orbits, and the theory of D-modules. The problem was motivated by the Landau–Ginzburg / Conformal Field Theory correspondence.
