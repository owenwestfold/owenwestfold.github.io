I am a mathematician and applied researcher, transitioning to AI alignment. My background is in algebraic geometry, singularity theory and geometric representation theory, and I am currently interested in Singular Learning Theory (SLT) and its applications to neural networks.

Singular Learning Theory

SLT is a mathematical theory of Bayesian statistical learning. I am currently reproving the main theorems in Watanabe's Grey Book in a coordinate free language — a working draft of my notes is here.

A core object of SLT, and key to the interpretation of the learning coefficient in terms of volume scaling, is the state-density function. I've written a note which explains how its asymptotic expansion is the distributional shadow of the V-filtration from D-module theory.

PhD

University of Melbourne, 2022. Supervised by Daniel Murfet. Thesis

I began my PhD as a budding algebraic geometer. My advisor, Dan Murfet, was working on a conjecture in mathematical physics known as the Landau-Ginzburg/Conformal Field Theory correspondence. Although I was not a physicist, both sides of this correspondence involved representation theory — a subject I had always liked — so I gladly joined the effort.

LG/CFT led me to representation theory of finite W-algebras in simply-laced type. These noncommutative algebras are quantisations of the Slodowy slice, and fit into a beautiful geometric picture involving nilpotent orbits and the Springer resolution of simple surface singularities. Using techniques from geometric representation theory (Ginzburg's characteristic cycle construction), I proved that the finite dimensional irreducible representations are all one-dimensional.
